Public Service Ads

Short Non-Fiction

  • Rewa Solar Park : Clean, Green, Cheap Energy

    60% of the daytime power requirements of Delhi metro are met by the Rewa Solar Park, situated 800km away in Madhya Pradesh. This 5 min film depicts key strategies that helped achieve a record low power tariff

  • Program Towards Elimination of Tuberculosis in India

    Tuberculosis kills more than 1,200 people in India every single day! That’s almost 500,000 deaths every year! What's even more alarming is that India has an estimated 500,000 missed TB patients who need to be screened, diagnosed and treated in a timely manner.Within just one year, District Koppal managed to pull off a dramatic turnaround to become one of Karnataka’s top performing districts in the fight against TB. This short film touches on some of their key strategies in the hope that it can offer inputs to other regions engaged in similar efforts.

  • SKILLING IN THE BFSI SECTOR

    In the BFSI (Banking, Financial Services and Insurance) Sector, a rapidly growing segment is Micro-finance. Rural women are given small loans without any collateral, and there are strict regulations on recovery methods. Loan Officers were thus delivered soft skills training through a mobile 'App' to ensure sensitivity in client dealings and effective recoveries..

  • SKILLING IN THE GREEN JOBS SECTOR

    Renewable energy assets like windmills and solar panels can last for 25-30 years, provided they are installed and maintained by suitably skilled manpower. It is difficult to get trainers with an industry background for grooming candidates to be industry-ready from day one. Hence, the Sector Skill Council for Green Jobs carefully deploys master trainers with adequate industry experience for mentoring trainers, who can in turn groom candidates across the country

  • SKILLING IN THE LOGISTICS SECTOR

    Mahindra Logistics, a leading service provider, couldn’t find enough men to operate forklifts in warehouses around the city of Pune. Logistics Skill Council helped execute a unique solution – local women were groomed as drivers. Arrangements for local training gave the women the opportunity to nurture their hidden potential.

  • Ten is the New Eight

    Until recently, India did not have enough secondary schools for its growing young population, nor enough teachers who could teach at the secondary level. Now, things have changed under India’s flagship secondary education program, the Rashtriya Madhyamik Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA).

  • Smart Public Transport

    Using the Intelligent Transport System (ITS) technology, the quality and convenience of bus operations across the rapidly growing city of Mysore is being improved, making it attractive for commuters to use public transport.

  • "India-Africa Knowledge Exchange on Skills"

    Encapsulation of a 3 day event, comprising exchanges between 26 ministerial delegates from Africa and their India counterparts,including field visits to skill centres.

  • A new beginning for visually impaired Girls

    All across India, the Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalaya (KGBV) program helps girls from underprivileged communities complete their formal schooling. However, in Jehanabad District, Bihar, a unique initiative is helping visually impaired girls catch up on lost learning.

  • Green Schools

    Under an initiative of the Gujarat State Government, students get a hands-on experience in nurturing the environment. They learn basic concepts such as bio-diversity, monitoring water quality, segregating waste, and using natural light and ventilation in buildings - all through simple solutions

  • Salt : Gift of the Earth

    film showcasing the journey of Nutrition International in supporting the Government of India to achieve Universal Salt Iodisation, reduce Iodine Deficiency Disorders and ensure a healthier population.

  • My New Life

    Puja, a young tribal girl from rural Rajasthan, yearns for a new life as she lives in a traditional society where girls stayed at home and were married off when they were young.

Long Non-Fiction

  • We must change this story

    A training and orientation film for doctors, paramedical staff and frontline providers, that documents maternal deaths and near-miss cases, which occurred while seeking treatment from public health facilities in Madhya Pradesh. Featuring intimate interviews with bereaved family members, the film sought to expose health service providers and health managers to the pain that every single death causes to the family. The aim was to motivate them to take preventive actions within their control to avert maternal deaths.

  • Sanjhi Sehat

    A documentation of the program which used the PLA approach for improving services and practices related to health, nutrition and WASH in interior tribal pockets of Madhya Pradesh.

  • Community Action For Health

    Depiction of changes brought about by the Community Action for Health (CAH) program under the National Rural Health Mission in Bihar. Dysfunctional health sub-centres were made functional, service providers stopped asking for informal payments for medicine and care, and quality of services improved significantly.

Fiction

  • Pag Pag Aage

    An infotainment musical drama of 35 mins that used Bihar's local theatre and TV artists to publicise guaranteed health rights and services under the NRHM. As a teaser, the title song was separately released, with glimpses from the film included in it

  • Hindola (1994)

    A gentle-paced telefilm with a mix of romance, humour and music, featuring Farooq Shaikh, Shekhar Suman, Smita Jayakar and Vikram Gokhale. Following positive viewer response and critical press acclaim, it was telecast numerous times for over two decades, offering a heart-warming trip down memory lane.

Animation

  • Beyond Boundaries: Understanding Airsheds and PM2.5

    A 5 minute animation film that introduces the concept of airshed management of air pollution in India and helps understand how PM2.5 is formed, how it travels and how it impacts health.

  • Building India’s Human Capital

    On average, Indian children born today can expect to attain only 49% of their full productive potential. This animation film emphasises human capital development for India to achieve its development goals.

Our team

Our initial efforts in the early 90s comprised social-awareness generation films, both fiction and documentaries, commissioned by numerous Government of India ministries. Abir also independently donned the director's cap for a range of fiction programs for television. A notable milestone was the telefilm 'Hindola', an endearing romantic comedy, featuring noted stars Farooq Shaikh, Shekhar Suman and Smita Jayakar. 'Hindola' received critical acclaim in the press (read press reviews) and was nominated in five categories for the prestigious Onida Pinnacle Awards in 1994.

Experience of the fiction & drama genre proved valuable as we moved on to produce development and advocacy films for the government and the development sector. Today, our films are appreciated for their in-depth understanding and depiction of program strategy, their emotional appeal and their judicious use of aesthetic visuals and music.

Our team

The Ishan Echoes team is led by writer-producer-director Abir Mishra, who brings to the table three decades of film-making experience, working with national and international agencies, covering diverse themes and genres. His interest in the development sector led him to do his Masters in Social Work in 2013. This helped him understand finer nuances of program strategy and advocacy. Proficient in English and Hindi, he interweaves appealing inflections of the dramatic genre while executing documentaries and advocacy films.

In accordance with the nature and scale of the project, Ishan Echoes handpicks its crew from its panel of professionals, reputed for their technical skill and zeal. Abir is personally involved throughout the process, right from the project-briefing, conceptualization, shooting and post-production up to the final delivery. This ensures that the organization and the Ishan Echoes team are on the same wavelength at every stage of execution.
